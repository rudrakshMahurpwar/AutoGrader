Exactly — relying too much on the similarity score alone can lead to inaccurate grading, especially when students express correct answers in varied ways.

Here are some practical ways to improve your automated grading system’s accuracy and balance between similarity and rubric feedback:

1. Combine Similarity Scores with LLM Rubrics More Intelligently

Use the similarity score as a rough filter or additional signal, not the final judgment.

For example, if similarity is low but rubric scores are high, trust the rubric more.

You can design a weighted scoring function like:

final_score = w1 * similarity_score + w2 * rubric_score_average


Tune w1 and w2 so the model relies more on rubric (LLM) scores.

2. Use Better or Fine-tuned Embeddings

Try embeddings more specialized for semantic similarity, e.g.:

Sentence-BERT fine-tuned on your domain or dataset.

OpenAI’s text-embedding-ada-002 (if you have access).

Domain-specific embeddings understand your subject better and reduce mismatch.

3. Expand Reference Answers

Have multiple high-quality reference answers for each question.

Compare student answers to all references and take the highest similarity.

This accounts for valid paraphrasing and diverse expressions.

4. Leverage LLMs More

Use LLMs not just for rubric scoring but for:

Paraphrase detection,

Grading using open-ended evaluation,

Or few-shot learning to evaluate answers.

This reduces dependence on raw embeddings.

5. Thresholds and Human-in-the-loop

Set thresholds for similarity below which you flag answers for manual review.

Gradually improve the system based on flagged edge cases.

6. Use Semantic Search Techniques

Instead of direct cosine similarity, consider techniques like cross-encoders that jointly encode pairs to compute similarity more accurately (though slower).

Summary

Don’t rely solely on similarity scores.

Use the rubric scores (from LLM) as the primary evaluation.

Use similarity scores for supplementary signals or consistency checks.

Improve embeddings and have diverse references.

Consider human review for ambiguous answers.
